01 / Branding / Packaging Design

Overload

Caffeinated Chaos

Energy Drink Brand Concept

Overview

Overload is a conceptual energy drink brand built around high intensity, speed, and controlled chaos. Inspired by action sports culture, the brand combines bold minimal design with aggressive visual elements to create something that feels fast, raw, and built for movement.

Creative Direction

The visual identity combines punk-inspired typography, high-contrast packaging, and caution-tape graphics to create a brand that feels explosive and rebellious while still remaining clean and modern.

Visual System

The matte black can design contrasts against sharp red accents and warning-inspired visuals, reinforcing the idea of high energy and overstimulation. Subtle sparks and industrial textures build an aggressive, fast-paced atmosphere without overwhelming the composition.

02 / Visual Identity / Event Branding

Lost & Found

Mapping Memory Through Music

Concept Music Festival

Overview

Lost & Found is a conceptual music festival where each day takes place in a different borough of New York City. The project explores how design can reflect location, culture, and identity while remaining connected through a single visual system.

Creative Direction

Inspired by NYC neighborhoods, transit systems, street culture, and music communities, the identity turns the city itself into part of the experience. Each borough carries its own visual voice while still belonging to a larger festival system.

Visual System

The visual language combines custom typography, borough-specific color palettes, map-based graphics, and layered urban textures. Structured city layouts are paired with distressed textures to create a balance between precision and chaos.

Poster SeriesFive posters representing the five NYC boroughs as one cohesive system.
MerchT-shirt design translating the borough-based identity into apparel.
TicketMetroCard-inspired festival pass using subway map language.
SocialNYC-inspired social graphics blending nostalgia and music culture.

03 / Concept / Packaging Design

Night Shift

Brewed For The Late Hours

Concept Beer Brand

Overview

Night Shift is a conceptual beer brand inspired by late nights, working-class culture, and gritty environments. The identity focuses on bold, minimal design with a strong visual system across multiple flavors.

Creative Direction

The brand was designed around endurance, routine, and life after dark. Industrial environments, blue-collar work culture, and late-night city energy shape a system that feels simple, bold, and slightly rough around the edges.

Packaging System

The three-can system uses consistent structure with flavor-specific variation. The variety pack brings all three flavors together through bold contrast, layered typography, and distressed illustration.

Amber LagerWarm caramel notes, crisp finish, and easy late-night drinkability.
Red AleRich malt profile, subtle bitterness, and steady balanced rhythm.
Midnight StoutDark, full-bodied, roasted, intense, and unapologetically bold.

04 / Visual Identity / EP Campaign

Art Club

Crystal Children

Band Identity / EP Campaign

Overview

Art Club is a Glasgow-based band blending Jazz, Afrobeat, Electronica, and Soul into a sound that feels both timeless and explosive. The visual identity for their EP Crystal Children reflects that same energy through bold color, abstract illustration, and expressive typography.

Creative Direction

The project explores a playful but striking visual language inspired by handmade artwork, surreal forms, and experimental music culture. Bright primary colors, organic shapes, and rough textures create something energetic, emotional, and unpredictable.

Visual System

The logo combines playful typography with handmade illustration, integrating abstract facial features and eye forms. The illustration style uses imperfect textures, hand-drawn elements, and surreal black shapes inspired by dream imagery, collage, and expressive mark-making.

05 / Concept Campaign / Art Direction

Nike x McDonald’s

I’m Lovin’ Every Step

Concept Collaboration Campaign

Overview

A conceptual collaboration between Nike and McDonald’s that blends fast food culture with performance and streetwear. The project explores how two global brands can merge through bold visuals, playful design, and cultural crossover.

Creative Direction

The campaign uses bright color, instantly recognizable food cues, and sneaker culture references to create a collaboration that feels unexpected, playful, and wearable.

Campaign System

The project expands across posters, a Big Mac-inspired Air Force 1, shoebox packaging modeled after a McDonald’s Big Mac box, streetwear merchandise, and a race car helmet extension that pushes the concept into speed and performance culture.

06 / Bar Branding / Logo / Packaging

Frat House

College Chaos, Branded

Concept Bar Brand

Overview

Frat House is a conceptual bar brand inspired by college party culture, dive bars, and late-night chaos. The project blends streetwear-inspired visuals, mascot branding, and immersive environmental graphics to create a playful nightlife identity.

Creative Direction

From the custom mascot logo to the door-style drink menu concept, every element was designed to feel energetic, humorous, and authentic to the atmosphere of a real frat-party bar experience.

Brand System

The logo combines nostalgic college-party culture with a bold cartoon house mascot inspired by vintage beer brands, Y2K skate graphics, and collegiate branding. The drink menu, beer can, and brand extensions translate the same chaotic personality into believable bar touchpoints.

LogoPlayful mascot identity with bright primary colors and expressive character design.
MenuDoor-style drink menu with humorous categories and late-night bar energy.
BeerCan packaging using bold typography, illustration, and swirl graphics.
EnvironmentImmersive system built around messy fun and nostalgic party culture.
